# Make the entry point mirror the pitch — surface the claims where the traffic lands

**When this applies:** you have a promo that names specific differentiators (a share post, launch tweet,
README badge, ad) AND a landing surface people hit when they follow it (a repo README, a product page, a
docs home, an onboarding screen).

**Principle:** the landing surface must surface the **same headline claims** the promo makes, up front.
If the post pitches three differentiators, the README has to show those three in the first screen — not
bury two of them in sub-files. A visitor following the promo verifies the pitch in ~10 seconds; if the
claims aren't visible there, the pitch reads as unbacked and they bounce. The detail can live deeper, but
a summary of each promised claim belongs at the entry point.

**Why:** claude-soul's share post led with three hooks — *principle over rule*, *7 delegation levels
(Tell→Delegate)*, and the *learning loop*. The README surfaced only the learning loop; the delegation
levels existed solely inside a sub-file (`.docs/principles/delegation-level-match-consumer.md`). A reader
clicking through from the post could confirm one claim of three on arrival — the other two looked like
marketing with nothing behind them, even though the repo fully implemented them.

**How to apply:**
- Before promoting, read the landing surface as a first-time visitor *following that exact promo*. For
  each headline claim, confirm it's visible without digging.
- If a promised claim is buried in a sub-file, lift a one-line summary to the entry point and link the
  detail — don't make the visitor go spelunking to validate the pitch.
- Keep the promo and the landing in sync when either changes; a claim added to the post needs a home on
  the page. Pairs with [[calibrate-to-the-real-audience]] and [[dont-expose-the-recipe]].
